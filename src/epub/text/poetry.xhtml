Creation

"The heavens declare the glory of God and the firmament showeth his handy work.”

v#O Earth, adore creative power, v#That made and gave to man as dower, v##This world of beauty rare, v#With hills and vales of verdant green, v#With rills and brooks of crystal sheen, v##Lovely beyond compare.

v#O Sun, bright ruler of the day, v#When first thy power thou did'st display, v##Earth must have shrank in fear, v#When like a meteor burst thy light, v#Turning to day the long, long night, v##With radiance wondrous fair.

v#Thou Moon, pale sister of the Sun, v#When he his daily work has done, v##Thou comest forth a queen; v#A queen in silvery robe adorned, v#With tiara of jewels formed, v##Of starry orbs unseen.

v#Ye twinkling stars of milder light, v#Though now ye gleam like sapphires bright, v##Across yon azure dome, v#The day will dawn, that last dread day, v#When from yon heaven you'll fall away, v##And man to Judgment come.

v#Thunder and Lightnings burst and gleam, v#Frightful and fierce to us they seem v##Rending the darkened sky. v#Like giants tread the thunder's peal, v#The vivid lightnings swiftly steal, v##And men in terror fly.

v#O filmy clouds, of purest white, v#With robes of gossamer cased in white, v##Ye floating waters pure, v#Sometimes to burst in cooling showers, v#Sometimes to deluge wintry hours v##With your relentless pour.

v#Thou beauteous Rainbow bursting forth, v#With varied hues encircling earth; v##The sign to Noah made. v#“I place amid the Clouds my Bow” v#To show that I will nevermore v##Deluge with angry flood.

v#Mountains and Hills whose snow capped tops v#The vast horizon overlooks, v##Pyramids strung and sure; v#Nor lightnings fierce nor earthquake shock v#Can ever sway, for firm as rock v##Ye ever will endure.

v#Thou Ocean vast, oftimes thy breast, v#Is calm and still as if at rest, v##Like one in quiet sleep; v#But soon in anger thou may'st roar, v#And madly toss from shore to shore, v##And human harvest reap.

v#Fountains and Rivulets so clear, v#That gush amid the valleys fair, v##With soft and mellow ring; v#As coming forth from glade and wood v#Your babblings whisper “God is good,” v##Ye make the vales to sing.

v#Now when all nature swells the song, v#When beast and birds the strain prolong, v##Shall man from praise refrain? v#Then would the rocks and hills proclaim, v#All nature crying out for shame, v#They who their Maker's image wear, v#Should shout and sing till rent the air v##With rhapsodies sublime.
